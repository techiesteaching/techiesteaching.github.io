Monica Packnett

(bio)

Monica Packnett

Let’s roll back about 5 years. The culmination of high school was fast approaching and I was confused about where life was going to take me. Having just received my ACT scores, I knew a 28 was good enough to get into say, Harvard, but my grades were not reflective of my intelligence or potential. My peers, mentors, teachers, and other influential figures in my life kept telling me that I would become something and was a force to reckoned with. Although aware of my own shortcomings, unbeknownst to me was a new beginning to reflect and understand my purpose and potential in the coming chapter of my life.
While in limbo between an unfinished high school diploma and the prospect of being able to attend college, Hampton University offered me a Presidential Scholarship to flourish at their “Home By The Sea.” I took the opportunity with open arms and welcomed a new beginning despite a lack of resolve from the previous chapter. Two years into a mathematics degree I returned to Chicago, downtrodden and tired, but still willing, and hungry. I don’t think I would be walking the current path if I hadn’t strayed from the previous one on which I had been set.
I was hungry to learn and needed a teacher. After working for a few big name box stores and a corporate umbrella I realized I dove in too early and hadn’t gotten swimming lessons yet. So, I backtracked to what I knew and returned to school. I had the ability to retract back in my book and finish my last semester of high school to receive an official Diploma. Upon completion, I enrolled in Olive Harvey Community College to fill in a few classes before entering a 4-year university. In tandem to continuing my education, I was looking for an internship to gain experience. Just in the nick of time, I received an opportunity to participate in Year Up, an IT workforce-training program where I’ve gained knowledge and skills in hardware, software, web development, and business communication to aid my journey in the corporate workforce.
